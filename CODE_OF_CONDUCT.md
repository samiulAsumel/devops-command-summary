## ðŸ‘¥ **ENFORCEMENT RESPONSIBILITIES**

### **Community Leaders**

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will:

- **Take appropriate and fair corrective action** in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- **Provide clear guidelines** for expected behavior
- **Lead by example** in demonstrating inclusive behavior
- **Respond promptly** to reported incidents
- **Maintain confidentiality** when appropriate
- **Document all enforcement actions**

### **Project Maintainers**

Project maintainainers have the right and responsibility to:

- **Remove, edit, or reject comments** that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- **Commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions** that are not aligned
- **Ban temporarily or permanently** any contributor for behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- **Communicate enforcement decisions** clearly and respectfully

## ðŸš¨ **ENFORCEMENT**

### **Reporting Process**

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported through the following channels:

- **Email**: conduct@devops-command-reference.com
- **GitHub Issues**: [Create a confidential issue](https://github.com/your-org/devops-command-summary/issues/new?assignees=&labels=conduct&template=conduct_report.md)
- **Slack/Discord**: Direct message to community moderators
- **Event Staff**: Report to event organizers at in-person events

### **Report Information**

When reporting, please include:

- **Your contact information** (optional, for follow-up)
- **Description of the incident**
- **Individual(s) involved**
- **Date and time of the incident**
- **Location where the incident occurred**
- **Any witnesses or evidence**
- **Desired outcome or resolution**

### **Confidentiality**

All reports will be handled with confidentiality and sensitivity. Personal information will be protected and only shared with necessary enforcement personnel.

### **Response Timeline**

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Initial Assessment**: Within 48 hours
- **Investigation**: Within 5 business days
- **Resolution**: Within 10 business days
- **Follow-up**: As needed

## ðŸ“‹ **REPORTING GUIDELINES**

### **For Victims**

If you are the victim of inappropriate behavior:

1. **Prioritize your safety** - Remove yourself from the situation if needed
2. **Document the incident** - Save screenshots, emails, or other evidence
3. **Report promptly** - Report as soon as you feel comfortable doing so
4. **Be specific** - Provide detailed information about what happened
5. **Request support** - Ask for help if you need it

### **For Witnesses**

If you witness inappropriate behavior:

1. **Intervene safely** - If safe to do so, intervene or support the victim
2. **Document what you saw** - Record details of the incident
3. **Report the incident** - Report what you witnessed
4. **Support the victim** - Offer support to those affected
5. **Follow up** - Check in on those affected

### **For Reporters**

When making a report:

1. **Be factual** - Stick to facts and avoid speculation
2. **Be thorough** - Provide as much detail as possible
3. **Be timely** - Report as soon as possible
4. **Be constructive** - Focus on solutions and improvement
5. **Be respectful** - Maintain respect for all parties involved

## âš–ï¸ **CONSEQUENCES**

### **Enforcement Actions**

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

#### **ðŸŸ¡ Correction (Community Impact: Low)**

**Behavior**: Unintentional or minor violations
**Consequences**:

- **Private warning** from community leaders
- **Clarification** of behavior expectations
- **Request** to correct behavior
- **Documentation** of the incident

#### **ðŸŸ  Warning (Community Impact: Medium)**

**Behavior**: Repeated or intentional violations
**Consequences**:

- **Official warning** from community leaders
- **Temporary removal** from community spaces
- **Requirement** to apologize or make amends
- **Mandatory** code of conduct training

#### **ðŸ”´ Temporary Ban (Community Impact: High)**

**Behavior**: Serious or harmful violations
**Consequences**:

- **Temporary ban** from community spaces (1-6 months)
- **Public acknowledgment** of violation
- **Requirement** to complete remediation program
- **Permanent record** of violation

#### **ðŸ”´ Permanent Ban (Community Impact: Severe)**

**Behavior**: Extremely harmful or illegal violations
**Consequences**:

- **Permanent ban** from all community spaces
- **Report to authorities** if illegal activity
- **Public disclosure** of ban (if appropriate)
- **Legal action** if necessary

### **Appeal Process**

Individuals who receive consequences may appeal by:

1. **Contacting the conduct committee** within 7 days
2. **Providing additional context** or information
3. **Requesting a review** of the decision
4. **Meeting with the committee** (if requested)
5. **Receiving a final decision** within 14 days

### **Restoration Process**

Individuals who have served consequences may request restoration by:

1. **Demonstrating understanding** of the Code of Conduct
2. **Showing changed behavior** over time
3. **Contributing positively** to the community
4. **Completing any required training** or programs
5. **Receiving approval** from community leaders
